Our problems will make us stronger

I read the caffeine withdrawal forums for 3 years. Its been over 6 months since I quit caffeine, but I never got better. My improvement was very subtle and would happen not by the day but could be felt (almost imperceptibly) every two weeks. I was not getting my work done. I lied over and over again about what I was accomplishing during the day. Luckily I was a able to work from home as I was not able to work a full day in an office.

I thought I was getting better then after 3 months I caught a cold and it knocked me out for 2 weeks. It was like starting from day one of caffeine withdrawal all over again. Laying in my bed that night the concept "adrenal fatigue" popped into my head. I read up on it and it described everything I was going through. I read everything I could about it but it was still hard to figure out what road to take. The online doctors who seem to know the most about it charge a lot of money which I dont have anymore. Even still with the help of a book, online articles and forum posts I patched together a plan and I started feeling better each day. However my improvement kind of plateaued so I tried more supplements and finally it seems to be working.

This process has taken away years off my life and cost thousands of dollars in medical bills. Particulary expensive was the overnight sleep study and the xyrem (ghb).

This site is my attempt to help the struggling.
